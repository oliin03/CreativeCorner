How It All Began

I never set out to be a graphic designer. My dream was to become a doctor—to wear that white coat, serve communities, and bring healing through medicine. But reality didn’t match the dream. University fees became walls I couldn’t climb, and what I thought was my path started to vanish. I had no tools, no studio, no fancy laptop. What I had was a phone and a fire inside.

That phone became my canvas. I began designing just to explore—to fill silence with color. What started out of necessity turned into obsession. Bit by bit, I taught myself layout, visual hierarchy, storytelling, and symbolism—right from a cracked screen and zero budget.

Today, I’m crafting a creative movement with Namibia Graphic Design Academy and building the civic-tech vision called NamHub. My origin wasn’t polished—but it was powerful. Every challenge became a chapter. Every design, a declaration that I wasn’t done yet.

“Started with nothing—but built something unstoppable.”

My Creative Timeline

  • 2022: Began experimenting with visual design and cultural symbolism in Namibia.
  • 2023: Started exploring digital storytelling, civic-tech ideas, and coding challenges.
  • 2025 (July): Launching a Namibia Graphic Design Academy (NGDA) 2 weeks free classes that will be having special rewards for you to enter the academy.
  • 2025 (Current): Developing NamHub, a civic-tech platform to unify digital public services across Namibia.
  • September 2025: Official Launching of NGDA’s, as we open our first ever Graphic Design Academy in Namibia
  • Beyond: Building educational kits, digital storytelling modules, and mentorship programs across Namibia.

My Values & Vision

  • Empowerment: Create digital education that uplifts underserved voices in Namibia.
  • Cultural Innovation: Design tools and visuals that celebrate local heritage in modern formats.
  • Accessibility: Build platforms that anyone can use—regardless of income or location.
  • Purposeful Creativity: Every design must solve problems, tell stories, and create change.
